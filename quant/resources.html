Books & Resources

1 min read · source: quant/resources.md

The two books sitting in the prep folder, plus the free material worth your remaining time. Notes on this site paraphrase classic problems; the books are the real drill sets.

The books (buy/borrow — not republished here)

  • Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability with Solutions — Frederick Mosteller. The canonical drill set; nearly every interview probability question is one of these fifty or a costume change of one. Goodreads
  • Heard on the Street: Quantitative Questions from Wall Street Job Interviews — Timothy Falcon Crack. Broader: probability, math, logic, and finance-flavoured questions with interview war stories. Site
  • Honorable mention: A Practical Guide to Quantitative Finance Interviews ("the green book") — Xinfeng Zhou — the standard next step if targeting Citadel-tier quant roles seriously.

Free and excellent

  • 3Blue1Brown — Bayes theorem — the geometric intuition for the toolkit page.
  • MIT 6.041 / 6.431 Probability (Tsitsiklis) — the lecture series if a concept refuses to click.
  • Brilliant.org probability wiki — quick worked examples in exactly interview register.
  • OSTEP is the OS analogue of these — linked from the OS pages.

How to drill (one week before)

  1. Re-derive, don't re-read: cover each answer on the brainteaser bank and refresher, answer aloud, then check.
  2. Do 10 Mosteller problems cold, timed at 5 min each. Wrong answers become flashcards.
  3. Practice the narration: state assumptions → tiny case → invariant/conditioning → answer → sanity check. The narration is what's scored.
