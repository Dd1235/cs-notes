Logic Puzzles — Ropes, Bridges, Prisoners

2 min read · source: quant/logic_puzzles.md

Interviewers don't want the memorized answer — they want to watch you find the lever. Each puzzle below names its lever explicitly.

Burning ropes

Two ropes each burn in 60 min, but non-uniformly. Measure 45 minutes.

Lever: light from both ends → half the time, regardless of non-uniformity.
Light rope A at both ends and rope B at one end. A finishes at 30 min; at that instant light B's other end — B's remaining 30 min halves to 15. Total 45.

Bridge and torch

Four people cross in 1, 2, 5, 10 min; bridge holds two; one torch; pair walks at the slower speed. Minimum total?

Lever: the two slowest must cross together, and fast people ferry the torch.
1&2 cross (2) → 1 returns (1) → 5&10 cross (10) → 2 returns (2) → 1&2 cross (2) = 17, not the greedy 19. Greedy (fastest escorts everyone) is the trap.

100 prisoners and a light bulb

Prisoners visited in arbitrary order, one at a time, one light switch, no other communication. Someone must eventually declare "all 100 have visited" — safely.

Lever: break symmetry — elect one counter.
Only the counter turns the light off (counting +1 each time he finds it on); every other prisoner turns it on exactly once in their life (only if it's off and they've never done it). When the counter reaches 99, declare. Slow but certain.

Ants on a pole

n ants on a 1 m pole walk at 1 m/min in fixed directions; on collision both reverse. Latest time until the pole is empty?

Lever: two ants bouncing is equivalent to two ants passing through each other (identities swap, positions don't care). So the answer is just the worst single-ant walk: 1 minute. Collisions were a decoy.

Two eggs, 100 floors

Find the highest safe floor with at most 2 eggs, minimizing worst-case drops.

Lever: equalize the worst case across the first egg's outcomes — make each successive first-drop gap one smaller: 14, 27, 39, 50, … → 14 drops worst case (n(n+1)/2 ≥ 100 → n = 14). The follow-up "k eggs" is the DP they actually want to discuss.

How to behave when stuck (this is graded too)

  1. Restate constraints out loud; 2. solve a tiny version (2 prisoners, 2 floors); 3. hunt for an invariant or symmetry; 4. name the trade-off you're optimizing. Silence is the only wrong move.

Seen at: D. E. Shaw, Citadel, and sporadically at Rubrik as "collaboration" probes.
