Random Walks & Gambler's Ruin

2 min read · source: quant/random_walks.md

One setup, a dozen interview questions: a drunk staggers ±1 on a line. Everything reduces to "absorbing walls and first-step equations".

Gambler's ruin

You have ₹a, opponent has ₹(N−a). Fair coin each round, ₹1 stakes, play until someone is broke.

  • Fair game: P(you win everything) = a/N — your share of the total money. Clean, memorable, provable by symmetry or by noting the walk is a martingale.
  • Expected duration: a·(N−a) rounds. Two players with ₹50 each: 2500 rounds — walks are slow.
  • Biased game (win prob p ≠ ½, r = q/p): P(ruin) uses the geometric ladder: P(reach N from a) = (1−rᵃ)/(1−r^N). Even a 51/49 edge compounds brutally over many rounds — this is why casinos always win.

Analogy: a fair random walk between two cliffs — your survival odds are exactly proportional to how far you start from your own cliff.

First-step analysis (the universal hammer)

Let Eₐ = expected rounds from state a. Then Eₐ = 1 + p·Eₐ₊₁ + q·Eₐ₋₁, with E₀ = E_N = 0. Every "expected time until…" walk question is this one template with different boundaries.

Facts worth having loaded

  • A fair walk returns to 0 with probability 1 in 1D and 2D, but only ~34% in 3D ("a drunk man finds his way home; a drunk bird may not" — Kakutani).
  • Expected distance from origin after n fair steps ≈ √(2n/π) — grows like √n, not n.
  • Reflection principle: paths from A that touch a barrier correspond 1-1 to reflected paths — the counting trick behind the ballot problem (see counting).

Drill

  1. Fair walk starting at 1, absorbing wall at 0 only — P(eventually hit 0)? (1 — but expected time is infinite. Both statements together is the interview answer.)
  2. You have ₹1, opponent ₹99, you win each round with p = 0.5 — your odds? With p = 0.55? (1%; then (1−r)/(1−r¹⁰⁰) ≈ 18% — edges matter more than bankrolls.)
  3. Frog on a 10-lilypad row, jumps L/R equally, pads 0 and 10 are absorbing, starts at 3 — expected jumps? (3·7 = 21.)

Seen at: Citadel and D. E. Shaw — ruin variants are near-guaranteed in quant-flavoured rounds.
