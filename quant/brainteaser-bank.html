Brainteaser Bank

2 min read · source: quant/brainteaser_bank.md

Ten classics, rapid-fire. Cover the answer column, commit to an answer out loud, then check. The one-line why is what you say in the interview.

The bank

1 · 25 horses, 5 tracks, no stopwatch — find top 3.
7 races. Five heats + final of heat-winners orders the winner; the only candidates for 2nd/3rd are 5 specific horses (2nd/3rd of winner's heat, 2nd of runner-up's heat, 3rd-place heat winner…) — race them once.

2 · 12 balls, one is off-weight (unknown direction), 3 weighings.
Split 4-4-4; each weighing has 3 outcomes, 3³ = 27 ≥ 24 possibilities — information-theoretically tight. Say that; deriving the full schedule live is rarely required.

3 · 3 jugs: 8 L full, 5 L, 3 L empty — split into 4+4.
BFS on states: (8,0,0)→(3,5,0)→(3,2,3)→(6,2,0)→(6,0,2)→(1,5,2)→(1,4,3)→(4,4,0). The meta-answer: jug puzzles are shortest-path search, and reachable amounts are multiples of gcd.

4 · Clock hands overlap how many times in 12 h?
11 — the minute hand laps the hour hand 11 times per 12 h, so overlaps are every 12/11 h ≈ 65 min 27 s.

5 · A duck in a circular pond, fox on the rim runs 4× swim speed.
The duck circles at radius < r/4 (where its angular speed beats the fox's), gets opposite the fox, then dashes: needs 3r/4 < π·r/4·(fox time). Escape is possible because angular speed ∝ 1/radius.

6 · Two trains 100 km apart, 50 km/h each; fly at 75 km/h bounces between.
Total collision time 1 h → fly travels 75 km. Sum the time, not the series (the von Neumann joke).

7 · Handshake parity: can 7 people each shake exactly 3 hands?
No — sum of degrees is odd (21), but handshakes contribute 2 each. Parity/invariant arguments kill "can it happen?" questions.

8 · Chessboard with two opposite corners removed — tile with 31 dominoes?
No — both removed squares are the same color; each domino covers one of each. Coloring invariant.

9 · 10 coins, 4 heads-up, you're blindfolded — split into two groups with equal heads.
Take any 4 coins as group A and flip all of them. If k of the 4 were heads, A now has 4−k heads — exactly what B has. Works with zero information.

10 · Monty-Hall-adjacent: 3 cards (BB, BW, WW), drawn card shows black face — P(other side black)?
2/3 — count faces, not cards: 3 black faces exist, 2 belong to BB.

Pattern index

Information bounds (2) · search over states (3) · relative motion (4, 6) · geometry of speed (5) · invariants/parity (7, 8, 9) · condition on faces not objects (10) · tournament structure (1).
