Citadel

2 min read · source: company_prep/citadel.md

Hedge fund + market maker (Citadel Securities). The OA is the wall: reported >85% filter rate, LC medium-hard→hard under 60–90 proctored minutes. After that: C++ depth and probability.

Pipeline

  1. HackerRank OA (the decider) — 2–3 problems, 60–90 min, camera/mic often on. Arrays/strings, hash-frequency, graphs/trees, DP, greedy — standard surface, brutal calibration and edge cases. Some tracks add math/probability MCQs.
  2. Phone screen(s) — live coding + rapid-fire fundamentals; probability teasers show up here.
  3. Onsite/superday — harder coding, C++ internals, concurrency, sometimes low-latency design discussion; behavioral is direct and short.

What they actually test

  • Speed with correctness — full marks need clean handling of edge cases on the first submit; partial credit thinking loses.
  • C++ internals — vtables, move semantics, memory layout, what allocations cost; "low-latency" flavour means knowing why things are slow.
  • Probability under pressure — expected value and conditioning questions delivered conversationally.
  • Concurrency literacy — atomics vs mutexes, false sharing gets mentioned in senior-ish loops.

Revise on this site

Quant section — all · Waiting times · Random walks · Memory layout & padding · vtables · Virtual functions · Multithreading in C++ · Locks internals

Practice prompts (representative, paraphrased)

  1. Timed set: two LC-hard problems in 70 minutes, no looking anything up, camera on — simulate the pressure, not just the topics.
  2. "You flip a fair coin until you get two heads in a row; I pay you the number of flips" — fair price? (Then: sell me the HT version.)
  3. What happens, allocation by allocation, when you push_back into a vector of strings 1M times? How do you make it fast?
  4. std::atomic counter vs mutex-guarded counter — when does each win, and what is false sharing?
