OOAD & UML Flashcards

5 min read · source: oops_cpp/flashcards/04_ooad_uml.md

U001

Tags: ooad

Prompt: What sources of complexity does OOAD try to manage?

Answer: Problem-domain complexity, development-process complexity, change pressure, many discrete states, and human cognitive limits.

U002

Tags: ooad

Prompt: OOAD manages complexity using which four broad tools?

Answer: Decomposition, abstraction, hierarchy, and models.

U003

Tags: complex-systems

Prompt: Name common traits of complex systems.

Answer: Hierarchical structure, relative primitives, separation of concerns, repeated patterns, and stable intermediate forms.

U004

Tags: object-model

Prompt: Major elements of the object model?

Answer: Abstraction, encapsulation, modularity, and hierarchy.

U005

Tags: object-model

Prompt: Minor elements of the object model?

Answer: Typing, concurrency, and persistence.

U006

Tags: abstraction, encapsulation

Prompt: Abstraction vs encapsulation?

Answer: Abstraction says what a thing is from the user's view. Encapsulation hides how it is represented and implemented.

U007

Tags: modularity

Prompt: High cohesion and low coupling?

Answer: High cohesion means related responsibilities stay together. Low coupling means modules depend on fewer details of other modules.

U008

Tags: modularity, smells

Prompt: Name three symptoms of poor modularity.

Answer: One class knows another's internals, one change forces many unrelated changes, and utility classes collect random unrelated behavior.

U009

Tags: objects

Prompt: State, behavior, identity in OOAD?

Answer: State is stored values. Behavior is supported operations. Identity is object sameness across state changes, distinct from value equality.

U010

Tags: relationships

Prompt: Match relationship to meaning: association, dependency, aggregation, composition, inheritance.

Answer: Association: objects know/use each other. Dependency: temporary use. Aggregation: weak whole-part, part can outlive whole. Composition: strong ownership, part dies with whole. Inheritance: is-a.

U011

Tags: relationships

Prompt: Aggregation vs composition in C++ representation?

Answer: Aggregation often uses non-owning references/pointers. Composition often uses value members or owning smart pointers such as unique_ptr.

U012

Tags: relationships

Prompt: Association vs dependency?

Answer: Association is a structural relationship where objects know/use each other over time. Dependency is temporary use, such as a function parameter or local variable.

U013

Tags: design-by-contract

Prompt: Define precondition, postcondition, and invariant.

Answer: Precondition: what must be true before a call. Postcondition: what is true after it returns. Invariant: what must always hold for a valid object.

U014

Tags: design-by-contract

Prompt: For a Fraction class, give a likely invariant.

Answer: Denominator is never zero; often also that the fraction is normalized/reduced if the class promises canonical form.

U015

Tags: class-discovery

Prompt: In noun/verb analysis, what do nouns, verbs, and adjectives often suggest?

Answer: Nouns suggest candidate classes or attributes. Verbs suggest responsibilities. Adjectives/adverbs suggest constraints, states, or qualifiers.

U016

Tags: class-discovery

Prompt: What questions filter candidate classes?

Answer: Is it a real concept or just a field? Does it own state? Does it have behavior? Does it protect an invariant? Is it stable under likely changes?

U017

Tags: uml

Prompt: What is UML, and when should you use a diagram?

Answer: UML is a modeling language. Use a diagram only when it answers a design question; do not draw UML as decoration.

U018

Tags: uml

Prompt: Structural vs behavioral UML diagrams?

Answer: Structural diagrams describe what exists, such as classes and relationships. Behavioral diagrams describe what happens, such as interactions, workflows, and state changes.

U019

Tags: uml, use-case

Prompt: What does a use case diagram capture?

Answer: External actors and user-visible system goals inside a system boundary, plus associations, includes, extends, and generalization.

U020

Tags: uml, use-case, edge-case

Prompt: Why are use cases not functions?

Answer: A use case represents a user-visible goal or service, not an implementation-level function call.

U021

Tags: uml, use-case

Prompt: Include vs extend in use case diagrams?

Answer: Include is mandatory reused behavior. Extend is optional or conditional behavior that augments a base use case.

U022

Tags: uml, class-diagram

Prompt: What belongs in a class diagram?

Answer: Class names, attributes, operations, visibility, relationships, and multiplicities at the right abstraction level.

U023

Tags: uml, class-diagram

Prompt: UML visibility symbols: +, -, #?

Answer: + public, - private, # protected.

U024

Tags: uml, class-diagram

Prompt: What does multiplicity mean? Decode 1, 0..1, *, 1..*.

Answer: Multiplicity says how many linked instances may participate. 1 exactly one, 0..1 optional one, * any number, 1..* one or more.

U025

Tags: uml, sequence

Prompt: What does a sequence diagram show?

Answer: Time-ordered messages between lifelines, with activation bars, calls/returns, creation/destruction, and time flowing top to bottom.

U026

Tags: uml, sequence

Prompt: How can a sequence diagram validate responsibility placement?

Answer: It reveals who initiates an operation, who owns the needed data, and whether too many messages cross module boundaries.

U027

Tags: uml, communication

Prompt: Communication diagram vs sequence diagram?

Answer: A communication diagram emphasizes object links and numbered messages; a sequence diagram emphasizes vertical time order.

U028

Tags: uml, activity

Prompt: What is an activity diagram good for?

Answer: Workflows, validation flows, business processes, decisions/merges, forks/joins, and swimlanes for responsibilities.

U029

Tags: uml, state-machine

Prompt: When should you use a state machine diagram?

Answer: When valid operations depend heavily on the object's current lifecycle state, such as order, ticket, or leave-request status.

U030

Tags: uml, protocol-state-machine

Prompt: What does a protocol state machine specify?

Answer: Valid operation sequences on an interface: which methods are legal now, what transitions they cause, and which calls are illegal in each state.

U031

Tags: lms, modeling

Prompt: Candidate classes for a leave-management system?

Answer: Employee, Approver, LeaveRequest, LeavePolicy, LeaveBalance, ApprovalWorkflow, Document, and Calendar.

U032

Tags: lms, modeling

Prompt: Responsibilities of LeaveRequest, LeavePolicy, ApprovalWorkflow, and Calendar.

Answer: LeaveRequest: dates, type, status, applicant. LeavePolicy: validates leave-type rules. ApprovalWorkflow: routes request to approver. Calendar: counts working days/holidays.

U033

Tags: lms, design

Prompt: How should different leave rules be modeled without a giant switch?

Answer: Put variable rules behind a LeavePolicy interface and use Strategy/policy objects, possibly created by a factory.

U034

Tags: lms, relationships

Prompt: Model these relationships: employee reports to employee; leave request belongs to employee; policy validates request.

Answer: Reporting is association/self-association. Request-to-employee is association or composition depending on persistence ownership. Policy validation is dependency or strategy composition.

U035

Tags: design

Prompt: When is inheritance a poor choice in OOAD?

Answer: When the relationship is not substitutable, behavior changes dynamically, shared code is the only motivation, or composition/policy objects would isolate variation better.

U036

Tags: design

Prompt: What is a good class interface expected to protect?

Answer: Its invariant, ownership boundaries, valid construction, meaningful operations, and stable abstraction without leaking representation.
